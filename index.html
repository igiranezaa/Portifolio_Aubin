Ingenzi Quadcopter Preliminary Design with Coaxial Rotors

Aug – Sep 2023

Overview

The Ingenzi Quadcopter is a pioneering UAV concept designed to push the boundaries of unmanned aerial systems in Rwanda. Equipped with coaxial rotors, it achieves exceptional thrust efficiency and stability by counter-rotating blade pairs that cancel torque, making it more stable and agile than conventional single-rotor designs.

Key Design Features

  • Coaxial rotor configuration for improved lift-to-weight ratio and torque cancellation
  • Ingenzi proprietary flight control technology for autonomous and semi-autonomous operation
  • Modular frame designed for rapid payload swapping across mission types
  • Optimized for hovering stability in high-altitude and wind-variable environments
  • Compact footprint enabling deployment in confined or urban spaces

Applications

  • Aerial surveillance and reconnaissance missions
  • Search and rescue operations in complex terrain
  • Agricultural monitoring and precision mapping
  • Infrastructure inspection in hard-to-reach areas

Tools & Methods

CATIA V5 for 3D modelling · ANSYS Fluent for aerodynamic simulation · Matlab for performance analysis · Open Rocket for flight trajectory estimation

Helicopter Preliminary Design and Analysis

Sep 2022 – Jan 2023

Overview

This capstone project involved the end-to-end preliminary design of a 15-seat, twin-engine Search and Rescue (SAR) helicopter using Matlab-driven sizing methods and aerodynamic analysis. The design was benchmarked against existing market rotorcraft to validate cost efficiency and performance targets.

Specifications

  • Capacity: 10 passengers + pilot + 4 crew/rescue personnel
  • Propulsion: Dual-engine configuration for redundancy and safety
  • Mission profile: Urban SAR with extended hover capability
  • Design goal: Affordable acquisition and operating cost vs. comparable rotorcraft
  • Range and endurance optimized for Rwandan topography and altitude conditions

Design Process

  • Preliminary sizing using momentum theory and blade element analysis
  • Weight estimation and center-of-gravity envelope calculation
  • Rotor blade geometry and tip speed optimization
  • Power required vs. available analysis across flight regimes
  • Market comparison with similar SAR platforms

Tools & Methods

Matlab for sizing and performance analysis · AutoCAD for technical drawings · CATIA V5 for 3D conceptual model · ANSYS for structural load estimation
